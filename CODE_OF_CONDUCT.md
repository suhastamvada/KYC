# Code of Conduct

This project exists to draft workable reforms, not to host a fight. The
standard here is the standard of a good committee room.

## Expected

- Critique proposals, evidence, and reasoning — never people.
- Cite sources for factual claims; accept corrections gracefully.
- Assume participants across regions, languages, and exam experiences are
  acting in good faith until shown otherwise.
- Keep discussion on education policy. Adjacent politics enters only where a
  proposal genuinely requires it.

## Not tolerated

- Personal attacks, harassment, or discriminatory remarks of any kind.
- Party-political campaigning, candidate promotion, or electioneering.
- Spam, off-topic flooding, or coordinated vote manipulation.
- Doxxing or sharing anyone's personal information.
- Misrepresenting sources or fabricating evidence.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or delete content that violates these
rules, and may block repeat offenders. Moderation actions on substantive
content (not spam) are noted in the discussion thread so they remain visible.

To report a violation, use GitHub's report feature or open a **Meta**
discussion (or contact the maintainer privately for sensitive matters).
